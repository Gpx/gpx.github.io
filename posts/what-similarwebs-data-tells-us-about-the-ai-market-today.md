I’ve been reviewing [Similarweb’s AI Global Trends for November 2025](https://www.similarweb.com/corp/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/attachment-Global-AI-Tracker-3.pdf), and the picture that emerges is quite consistent across categories. **Most AI tools are losing traffic**, and in some cases the drop is dramatic. Apart from Gemini, even the biggest players are trending down.

Code completion and DevOps tools like Cursor, Lovable and v0 are seeing large percentage drops. v0 in particular is down around ninety five percent. Base44 is one of the few exceptions, but it’s still early in its lifecycle and its growth has already begun to slow.

{{ '/images/what-similarwebs-data-tells-us-about-the-ai-market-today/12kw-change-devops-code-tools.png' | figure: 'DevOps & Code Completion Tools: 12-Week Change in Traffic' }}

This pattern repeats almost everywhere. Character and Chat AI, Design and Image Generation, Writing and Content Generation, Video, Voice and Music Generation, Automation and Travel all show a downward trend overall, with only a few individual tools bucking it.

{{ '/images/what-similarwebs-data-tells-us-about-the-ai-market-today/devops-code-completion-tools-traffic.png' | figure: 'DevOps & Code Completion Tools: Traffic Comparison' }}

Some of these curves strongly resemble the _Gartner Hype Cycle_. It feels like we’re moving from the _Peak of Inflated Expectations_ into the _Trough of Disillusionment_. In practice this means the hype is fading and people are no longer willing to spend money on promises. They want tools that reliably deliver value.

{{ '/images/what-similarwebs-data-tells-us-about-the-ai-market-today/garner-hype-cycle.png' | figure: 'Gartner Hype Cycle By <a href="By Olga Tarkovskiy - Own work, CC BY-SA 3.0, https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?curid=27546041">Olga Tarkovskiy</a>', 'Gartner Hype Cycle' }}

From my point of view **this is a healthy shift**. It gives us space to focus on the areas where AI truly works today and filter out the noise. Real use cases can emerge more clearly once the hype cools down.

I’d love to hear how others see this trend and whether you’re noticing the same pattern in your own work.
